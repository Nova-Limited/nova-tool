# Nova Phone Call Playbook - Day 3 + Day 10

**Drafted Mon 25 May 2026 evening.** Companion to `managing-agent-phone-cadence.md`. Scripts for Day 3 (Call 1) and Day 10 (Call 2) phone touches in the cold sequence, plus objection handlers, voicemail templates, and Contact Log workflow.

Applies to all three tracks: Standard, Multi-Site, Managing-Agent.

---

## 1. Operating principles

Five rules that hold across every call:

1. **Lead with the email reference.** "I sent you an email on Tuesday" is the strongest gatekeeper bypass and the strongest opener. The prospect has already encountered Nova's name, even if briefly.

2. **MD-personally-calling is your advantage. Don't apologise for it.** No "I know this is unusual" framing. Confident, direct, expert-led. You ARE the most experienced person in the building.

3. **Time-respectful framing wins.** Every opener offers two paths: "two minutes now" OR "when's better?" This shows respect, removes pressure, and (paradoxically) makes people more likely to engage now.

4. **The goal is the next conversation, not the close.** Day 3 success is "meeting booked or interest signalled." Day 10 success is "meeting booked or specific objection raised." Either is forward motion.

5. **Acknowledge → Pivot → Next step.** Every objection response: acknowledge the concern, pivot to a discovery question, propose a specific micro-commitment. Never argue, never push.

## 2. Day 3 Call 1 - "Did the email land?"

### 2.1 Context

3 days after Email 1 (the door opener). Email 1 has either:
- Been read and ignored
- Been skim-read and parked
- Sat in inbox unread
- Been read by an assistant who didn't forward

Your job: turn any of those into a 5-minute conversation.

### 2.2 Opener (default)

When prospect answers directly:

> "[Name]? Jamie Gibbs from Nova. I sent you an email Tuesday about your [portfolio shape - 'multi-site portfolio' / 'commercial roof' / 'industrial estate']. Two-minute question if you've got it - if not, when's better?"

Why this works:
- Names the prospect (confirms you have the right person)
- Identifies you cleanly (no surname-only confusion)
- References the email (legitimises the call, gives them a memory hook)
- References specific content (proves you know who they are, not a blanket call)
- Offers exit ramp upfront ("when's better?") which signals respect

### 2.3 Opener variants by sector

**Multi-site industrial/logistics prospect:**
> "[Name]? Jamie Gibbs from Nova. I sent you an email Tuesday about your [X] sites - the [TotalRoofArea]m² roof portfolio. Two-minute question on the figures if you've got it?"

**Builders Merchant:**
> "[Name]? Jamie Gibbs from Nova. I sent you an email Tuesday about your [X]-branch network and a roof refurb plus solar package. Two minutes on what we put together?"

**Managing-Agent contact:**
> "[Name]? Jamie Gibbs from Nova. I sent you an email Tuesday about a landlord-led roof and solar package across your managed portfolio. Two minutes worth on how the agent fee position sits in the structure?"

### 2.4 If they engage

> "Great. Quick context: we work on commercial roof refurb and rooftop solar across [portfolio type] like yours. The email had figures - did anything stand out, or were you wanting more detail before the conversation makes sense?"

This:
- Restates Nova's offer in one line
- Asks an open question that reveals where they are
- Offers two paths: engage on figures OR ask for more detail (both forward motion)

### 2.5 If they say "send me more info"

This is the most common deflection. Don't accept blindly.

> "Of course, I can send you a one-page summary. To make sure it's actually useful and not another generic deck, can I ask one quick question? Is [the relevant trigger - MEES compliance / asset documentation / energy cost reduction] something currently on your radar at [CompanyName], or is roof and solar work just not on the priority list right now?"

This:
- Honours the request (will send something)
- Buys 60 seconds of discovery
- Reveals whether they're genuinely interested or politely brushing you off
- Lets you tailor what you send (or save the effort if they're a "no")

If they confirm interest, follow with:
> "Useful, thanks. I'll send a one-pager focused on [their stated concern]. Could I pencil a 15-minute call for Thursday next week to talk through whether the numbers actually work for [CompanyName]?"

### 2.6 If they say "we have a contractor"

> "That makes sense - most of the teams we end up working with already had someone. Can I ask what they're handling - is it ongoing maintenance, or are you looking at major works in the next 18 months too?"

This:
- Removes defensiveness ("not trying to replace your guy")
- Discovers whether they have an actual capital-works supplier vs a reactive maintenance contractor
- Most regional roofers don't do solar - that's your wedge

If they have a maintenance contractor only:
> "Most of our work sits alongside an existing maintenance arrangement rather than replacing it - the capital projects (refurb + solar combined) tend to need someone different. Would 15 minutes be useful to walk through what that combined position looks like for [CompanyName]?"

### 2.7 If they say "not interested"

Don't push. One soft re-frame, then close gracefully.

> "Fair enough. Out of interest - is that 'not interested in talking to a roofing contractor' or 'not interested right now because of timing'? The reason I ask is the second one usually has a date attached we should respect."

If still firm no:
> "Understood. I'll close the file. If anything changes - lease renewal, EPC issue, anything triggers a roof question - my direct line is 0203 479 2994. Have a good day, [Name]."

Then: **set their Stage to "Not Interested - Closed" in Companies (or Email Finder for Managing-Agent)** - stops all future emails and calls.

### 2.8 If they say "I'm busy / wrong time"

> "Totally understand. When IS a better time - later today, tomorrow morning, or just want me to follow up next week?"

This locks down a specific commitment, not a vague "later."

### 2.9 Gatekeeper bypass

If you reach reception/PA:

> "Morning. Jamie Gibbs, MD at Nova - I sent [Name] an email on Tuesday about [CompanyName]'s [portfolio/sites/branches] and need a 60-second follow-up with him/her. Is he/she free, or should I call back later?"

Why this works:
- States your title (MD, not salesperson)
- References specific prior contact (you're not cold)
- Specific time ask (60 seconds, not "a few minutes")
- Offers the gatekeeper a useful path ("call back later")

If they ask "what's this regarding":
> "It's a follow-up to my email Tuesday about [specific topic - the roof portfolio / the solar package]. He/she'll know the context."

Don't elaborate further. The more you explain, the more they screen.

### 2.10 Voicemail (if you reach it)

Keep it short. 25-30 seconds maximum.

> "Hi [Name], Jamie Gibbs from Nova, MD - calling to follow up the email I sent you Tuesday about [CompanyName]'s [portfolio shape]. Won't keep you. Best to call me back on 0203 479 2994 or I'll try again Friday morning. Cheers."

Why this works:
- Names you twice (your name, prospect's name) - sounds personal
- References specific prior contact
- Doesn't pitch on the voicemail (the goal is callback, not sale)
- Specific next-action commitment (you WILL call back Friday)
- Casual sign-off ("cheers") not corporate ("kind regards")

### 2.11 Day 3 success ladder

In order of preference:

1. Meeting booked for next week
2. Specific callback time agreed
3. Permission to send a one-pager + callback time agreed
4. Specific objection raised that we can address in Email 3
5. "Not interested" but professional close - Stage set to Not Interested - Closed
6. Voicemail left, no response
7. Gatekeeper blocked, no progress

Anything 1-4 is a win. 5 is a clean close. 6 means try again Day 10. 7 means email pivot.

## 3. Day 10 Call 2 - "Walk through the figures"

### 3.1 Context

3 days after Email 2 (the figures email). Different from Day 3 because:
- They've now had two emails from you
- Email 2 had specific financial figures (RoofCost, SolarCost, Year1Saving, PaybackPeriod)
- They've either engaged with Email 2 or pointedly not engaged with Email 2

Your job: get them to react to the figures, either positively or with a specific objection you can address.

### 3.2 Opener (default)

> "[Name]? Jamie Gibbs from Nova. Sent you the figures last Tuesday - the [RoofCost] refurb plus [SolarCost] solar, [Year1Saving] year one saving on a [PaybackPeriod]-year payback. Wanted to walk through assumptions if you've got two minutes."

Why this works:
- References specific numbers from Email 2 (proves you've personalised the outreach)
- Frames the call as "walk through assumptions" not "do you want to buy" (lower pressure)
- The numbers do the heavy lifting - prospect knows you've done work

### 3.3 Opener variants

**If Day 3 call connected and meeting was deferred:**
> "[Name]? Jamie Gibbs from Nova. Following up our conversation last week - sent you the figures Tuesday. Did they sharpen the picture or raise more questions?"

**If Day 3 call hit voicemail:**
> "[Name]? Jamie Gibbs from Nova. Tried you last week, sent you the figures Tuesday in case you didn't get the voicemail. Worth two minutes on what they mean for [CompanyName]?"

**For Managing-Agent (different framing):**
> "[Name]? Jamie Gibbs from Nova. Sent you the figures Tuesday on the landlord-led package - what works on a typical 1,500m² unit. Wanted to walk through the agent fee structure quickly if you've got two minutes."

### 3.4 If they say "the numbers seem high"

This is actually engagement - they've read the email.

> "Fair observation - they're indicative numbers based on desktop analysis. The real numbers tend to come back lower after drone survey because we see exactly which roof areas are productive and which aren't worth solar. Could we get a free drone survey booked, then talk again once you've got accurate figures?"

This:
- Validates their concern without backing down
- Reveals the desktop-vs-drone delta as a feature, not a flaw
- Pivots to the free drone survey as the natural next step (low commitment, high information)

### 3.5 If they say "we don't have budget right now"

> "Understood. Out of interest - is the issue this calendar year, or is roof + solar generally not on the capital plan? The reason I ask: if you're looking at any roof works in the next 18-24 months, solar piggy-backed on those works typically costs 40% less than installing them separately later, so the budget conversation works better when both are on the table at once."

This:
- Removes pressure ("understood")
- Discovers whether budget is now-only or never (huge difference)
- Educates without lecturing (the 40% cost saving from combined works is genuine)
- Pivots to a discovery question

### 3.6 If they say "send me a proposal"

> "I can do that. The accurate proposal comes after drone survey - the desktop figures sharpen up considerably once we have real roof data. Could we book a 30-minute drone visit first? It's free, takes about an hour on site, and gives you actual figures to evaluate rather than indicative ones."

This:
- Honours the request (proposal coming)
- Introduces the drone survey as the gate to accurate figures
- Frames the drone survey as benefit to THEM (accurate figures) not Nova (sales tool)

### 3.7 If they say "show me your previous work"

This is a credibility question. Memory guidance: don't fabricate, don't lean on company history.

> "That's a fair ask. I'd rather not pitch case studies in a phone call - they'll all sound impressive. The most credible thing I can offer is a 15-minute call with one of our existing clients to ask them directly about working with us. Or we do a free drone survey at your site and you assess the proposal on its merits. Which works better for you?"

This:
- Acknowledges the legitimacy of the question
- Pivots to a stronger proof point (peer reference) than a polished case study
- Offers two paths, both forward motion

### 3.8 If they say "we don't trust solar paybacks"

> "Sensible scepticism - I'd push back on confident solar numbers too. The figures I sent are based on UK published data: kWh per kWp per year is well-established, electricity rates are visible, panel costs are competitive. The variable is consumption - whether you actually use the power on-site or export it. Site-specific consumption data is what the drone survey plus a half-hour meter review confirms. Want to put that in motion?"

This:
- Aligns with their scepticism rather than fighting it
- Defends the methodology with specifics
- Identifies the actual variable (consumption profile) not generic solar maths
- Pivots to a specific discovery step

### 3.9 If they say "I'm not the right person"

> "Got it. Who at [CompanyName] is the right person to talk to about commercial roof and solar capital projects? Estates director, head of property, facilities lead - someone like that?"

If they name someone:
> "Thanks - is [Name] the best contact, or should I reference our conversation when I reach out so they know I'm not cold?"

This:
- Gets the name AND permission to use it (huge legitimacy boost)
- "Reference our conversation" is the magic phrase - they've effectively pre-warmed you to the decision-maker

### 3.10 Day 10 voicemail

> "Hi [Name], Jamie Gibbs from Nova again. Sent you figures Tuesday on the [RoofCost] refurb plus solar package for [CompanyName]. Won't keep adding to your inbox - if those numbers don't make sense for you right now that's fine, just call me back on 0203 479 2994 to close the file. Otherwise I'll try once more next week. Cheers."

Why this works:
- Specific financial reference (proves real engagement)
- "Close the file" framing - gives them a clean exit, removes pressure to respond
- Sets expectation of one more attempt then close (respects their time)

### 3.11 Day 10 success ladder

1. Meeting / drone survey booked
2. Named the right decision-maker with permission to reference
3. Specific objection raised that needs Email 3 (risk angle) to address
4. Re-engagement triggered (will read Email 3 + Email 4 properly)
5. "Not interested - closed" clean close
6. Voicemail / gatekeeper, no progress

## 4. Universal scripts

### 4.1 "Are you trying to sell me something?"

> "Yes - eventually. Right now I'm trying to find out whether what we do is even relevant to [CompanyName]. Most of these calls end in 'not a fit' and that's fine. The 10% that ARE a fit go to a free survey, and a fraction of those become work. So at this stage, no pressure - just trying to work out which group you're in."

### 4.2 "How did you get my number?"

> "Companies House for the company, Google for the office number, [or] our property data tool flagged [CompanyName]'s portfolio. Public sources only - happy to send you a 60-second explainer if you want one."

Honest, not defensive. The Find Email + /places workflow is all public-data based.

### 4.3 "I'll think about it and get back to you"

The classic polite brush-off. Don't accept without a follow-up commitment.

> "Of course. Out of interest, what's the rough timeline - this week, next month, next quarter? The reason I ask: if it's months away I'll close the file and email you in [Q3], if it's sooner I'll hold the line. What works?"

### 4.4 "Send me your terms / certifications / insurance details"

Per memory: don't fabricate accreditations. We have NO NFRC/CHAS/ISO/PI.

> "I can send you what we have - 22 years of sector experience documented, construction law qualifications, our standard terms. We don't carry every industry trade body certificate - some we work alongside trade partners who do. Worth a 15-minute call to talk through what specifically you'd need for [CompanyName] to engage us?"

This:
- Honest about the gap
- Reframes to Nova's strengths (experience + qualifications)
- References the trade partner model (memory entry 14)
- Pivots to discovery

## 5. Contact Log workflow

After every call, log in Contact Log (`tblDO47IagkE4W6Lp`):

| Field | What to enter |
|---|---|
| Prospect | Company name |
| Contact Name | Person you spoke to (or "Voicemail" / "Gatekeeper") |
| Email | Their email if known |
| Method | "Phone" |
| Subject | "Day 3 Call 1" or "Day 10 Call 2" |
| Outcome | One of: Meeting booked / Callback scheduled / Spoke - interested / Spoke - objection / Voicemail / Gatekeeper / No answer / Not Interested - Closed |
| Notes | Brief summary - what was said, what they asked, anything to remember |
| Date | Today |
| Follow Up Date | If applicable, when to retry |
| Completed | Tick if no further action needed for this touch |

### 5.1 Required Outcome options

Verify Contact Log Outcome singleSelect has these:
- Meeting booked
- Callback scheduled  
- Spoke - interested
- Spoke - objection
- Voicemail left
- Gatekeeper blocked
- No answer
- Not Interested - Closed
- Sent to colleague

If any missing, add via Airtable UI (no MCP support for singleSelect option changes).

### 5.2 Updating Companies / Email Finder

After logging the call:
- Update Companies.Stage (Standard / Multi-Site) or Email Finder.Stage (Managing-Agent) per the Outcome
- Update Last Call Date field (when added per managing-agent-phone-cadence.md schema)
- If "Not Interested - Closed", set Stage to that explicitly

## 7. Anti-patterns - what NOT to do

- **Don't ask "is this a good time?"** It primes a "no". Use "two minutes if you've got it" instead.
- **Don't apologise for calling** ("sorry to bother you"). Confident, expert-led - you're delivering value, not interrupting.
- **Don't pitch the company history.** Per memory, Nova's predecessor failed - leave that alone. Lead with your credentials (22 years, LLM, Royal Marines if context fits).
- **Don't fabricate scale.** "Our team" is fine (trade partners count) but never invent staff numbers, accreditations, or named contractors.
- **Don't use "competition in the office" framing** (discussed Mon 25 May). MD-personally-calling is genuinely better.
- **Don't use em dashes in any follow-up emails after a call.**
- **Don't promise call-backs you won't make.** If you say "I'll call Friday morning," call Friday morning. Trust dies fast on missed callbacks.
- **Don't assume the gatekeeper is the enemy.** Treat them as a useful filter. They often know who's the right contact.
- **Don't talk price on the phone.** Always pivot to drone survey first - desktop figures aren't accurate enough to defend on a call.

---

## 8. Closing thought

Phone calls have a different physics from email. You can hear the engagement (or lack of it). Use that.

If they sound rushed, end the call short and follow up in writing.
If they sound engaged, push for the meeting commitment before hanging up.
If they sound sceptical, lean into the scepticism with specific data - don't oversell.
If they sound annoyed, close gracefully and never re-contact unless they reach out first.

The 22 years of sector experience is your structural advantage. Most people on the other end of these calls won't have that. Let it show, don't hide it.
